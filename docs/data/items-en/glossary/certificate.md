## Definition

A digital Certificate is an electronic document used to verify a device's identity and enable encrypted communication. In an MDM environment, certificates are used to prove who the device is (authentication), establish secure connections (TLS), and sign apps. If a certificate expires, the device may lose its ability to connect to Wi-Fi or communicate with the management server.
